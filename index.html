About

The human
behind the queries

I'm a Data Engineer who ended up here because I genuinely cannot stop asking "wait, why does this take so long?" and "who actually uses this report?" Based in Indianapolis, IN, I've spent the last few years as a Consultant — building data systems that work across public health agencies, insurance firms, energy markets, and enterprise operations.

"If your pipeline runs but nobody trusts the output,
did it really run?"

My work sits at that rare intersection of deep technical fluency and the ability to explain it to a room full of people who'd rather be anywhere else. I've designed ETL architecture from scratch, cut reporting latency in half, and once trained a non-technical team so well they started catching their own data anomalies. That last one felt like a small victory for humanity.

Off the clock, I'm probably over-engineering a personal project, judging a hackathon, or trying to convince someone that data quality is actually interesting if you frame it right. Spoiler: it usually works.
